Wildlife Photography

Wildlife photography is a captivating art that requires patience, dedication, and a deep connection with the natural world. Sameera's remarkable journey in this field led him to the enchanting heart of the Sri Lankan rainforest, Singharaja. This pristine UNESCO World Heritage site, with its lush canopy and rich biodiversity, served as the backdrop for Sameera's awe-inspiring wildlife photography expedition.

Armed with his camera and guided by a profound love for nature, Sameera spent several months immersing himself in the rainforest's dense undergrowth and tranquil streams. He patiently waited for elusive creatures, from the vibrantly colored birds that danced through the trees to the secretive leopards that prowled the forest floor. His relentless pursuit of the perfect shot involved countless early mornings and late nights, studying animal behaviors and adapting to the ever-changing conditions of the rainforest. After months of dedicated effort, Sameera's persistence paid off when he captured a series of breathtaking wildlife photographs that portrayed the Singharaja rainforest in all its natural splendor. His images, showcasing the diverse flora and fauna of the forest, not only demonstrated his technical prowess but also conveyed the deep sense of reverence and conservation that guided his work. In recognition of his outstanding contribution to the world of wildlife photography, Sameera was honored with the prestigious "Wildlifer 2020" award, a testament to his dedication and the powerful narratives he shared through his lens. His work not only celebrated the beauty of the Singharaja rainforest but also served as a call to action, urging viewers to protect these fragile ecosystems for future generations.







Landscape Photography

Sameera's foray into landscape photography brought him to the iconic rock fortress of Sigiriya in Sri Lanka, a place steeped in history and natural beauty. The towering rock formation, enveloped by lush greenery and crowned by ancient ruins, provided the perfect canvas for Sameera's creative vision.

After extensive fieldwork and meticulous post-processing, Sameera's portfolio of Sigiriya's landscapes was nothing short of breathtaking. His images showcased the rock fortress at different times of the day, capturing its mystique at dawn, its grandeur under the midday sun, and its tranquil beauty during twilight. This remarkable collection earned Sameera the esteemed "Capture of the Year 2021" award, a recognition of his exceptional talent in bringing out the timeless charm of Sigiriya through his lens. His work not only celebrated the historical significance of the site but also served as an invitation for viewers to immerse themselves in the enchantment of this ancient marvel.







Portrait Photography

While on his photography journey in Sri Lanka, Sameera had the serendipitous opportunity to capture the captivating essence of a Turkish woman named Leyla, who had traveled to the island to immerse herself in its culture. Leyla's unique blend of grace and vivacity drew Sameera's attention, and he approached her with a proposal for a portrait photography session.

Under the soft, golden glow of the Sri Lankan sunset, Sameera photographed Leyla against the backdrop of a historic temple. Her eyes sparkled with a mixture of curiosity and wonder, mirroring the charm of the island that had captivated her. The vibrant colors of her traditional Turkish attire stood in beautiful contrast to the serene surroundings, creating a portrait that celebrated both her individuality and the cultural diversity that makes Sri Lanka such a unique place.

The resulting photograph of Leyla not only captured her timeless beauty but also conveyed the spirit of cross-cultural connection and exploration. The image resonated with the judges at the prestigious "Colombo Pic Carnival," and Sameera was awarded first place in the portrait photography category. This accolade not only celebrated his talent in capturing the human spirit through his lens but also highlighted the power of photography in bridging cultures and bringing people together. Leyla's portrait became a symbol of the rich tapestry of stories that can be woven through the art of portrait photography, transcending borders and boundaries.







Close-up Photography

Sameera's close-up photography talent shone brilliantly when he decided to focus his lens on the intricacies of a brown eyeball, an image that would ultimately earn him the esteemed title of "Best Closeup of the Night 2020."

With meticulous attention to detail, Sameera embarked on a quest to unveil the hidden universe within the human eye. He set up a controlled environment with precise lighting, creating the perfect conditions to capture the eye's intricate textures, mesmerizing patterns, and the play of light on its surface. The result was nothing short of mesmerizing: the brown iris seemed to swirl with a galaxy of its own, while the fine details of the pupil and the delicate network of blood vessels emerged as a testament to the marvel of human biology.

The photograph was an instant sensation at the photography competition, leaving both judges and spectators in awe of its artistry and technical precision. Sameera's image not only celebrated the beauty and complexity of the human eye but also demonstrated his mastery of close-up photography as a medium for revealing the extraordinary within the ordinary. It was a reminder that even in the most commonplace subjects, there is a world of wonder waiting to be explored through the lens, and it earned Sameera the well-deserved title of "Best Closeup of the Night 2020."
